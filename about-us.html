About Us

We, at Nature’s Miracle, are committed to produce fruits & vegetables matching the highest standards of quality prevailing in the international market. With the glocalization of consumer information and their quality expectations from products and services, our team is fully committed to developing all round capabilities in bringing high-quality fruits and vegetables to consumers across India. Ours is a first of its kind Glass Greenhouse of this scale in India. We are Global GAP Certified Growers and follow protocols that match the international standards.

Constantly innovating and adapting ourselves to anticipate the future needs of our customers, we clearly see the importance of growing our vegetables in a pollution free and sustainable environment. This vision inspired us to travel, research and train our team intensively with the world’s leading authority in hydroponics in the Netherlands. With growing levels of contamination in the soil, water and environment, we chose to make good health for families our top priority.

We offer nutrition packed easy-to-carry snack veggies enabling a healthy ‘on-the-go’ lifestyle for all age groups. In due course, it has become apparent that our products carry themselves well even when processed in various cuisines in a variety of formats.

A 4-hectare glasshouse complex at our Greater Noida farm is an endeavour to create and promote healthy living through healthy eating. This is our commitment to the long-term health of the future generation.

Ideal Climate and Nutrition

Our fully automated greenhouse is equipped with the latest and most advanced technology to control and optimize temperature, humidity, optimal nutrition and freuqncy for irrigation and many more parameters to create an ideal growing micro envionrment for the respective crops which in turn ensures quality and consistent produce.

Zero Wastage

KNOW MORE

Zero Wastage

We follow a zero wastage policy and reuse water after UV disinfection. In this way, our hydroponic farm only uses 10% of the water compared to a normal farm.

Furthermore, since it is a closed system, nutrients do not leach away – therefore making it possible to use a fraction of the fertiliser used by a regular farm.
