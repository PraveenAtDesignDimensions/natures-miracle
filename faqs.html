What is Hydroponics?

Hydroponics, simply defined, is the growing of plants without soil in a water and fertilizer solution containing the necessary nutrients for plant growth. It is not a new science. Work in this area was being done by researchers as early as the 1600's. In the early 1930's, W. F. Gericke of the University of California did laboratory experiments in plant nutrition for the commercial scale. "HYDROPONICS" the word was derived from two Greek words, "hydro", meaning water and "ponic" meaning labour or literally, water working.

With the development of plastics, hydroponics took a large step forward and is now a widely accepted method of producing certain specialty crops such as tomatoes, lettuce, cucumbers and peppers. Other crops that can be grown by this method include herbs, foliage plants and flowers.

Hydroponics has significant advantages. They include:

  • Superior taste, quality, appearance, uniformity and extended shelf life of hydroponic vegetables.
  • No sterilization of growing media is required and plant nutrition is easily and completely controlled within the nutrient tanks.
  • No weeds, no cultivation, no soil borne diseases or insects. It allows for uniform water availability in plants.
  • Closer plant spacing is possible because no large machinery is used inside the greenhouse.
  • Use of biological controls including beneficial insects and safe methods of insect control are possible in a controlled environment system.
  • It is believed, by many, that Controlled Environmental Agriculture is the answer to many of the difficulties that are often associated with outdoor production.
  • Terrestrial plants may be grown with their roots in the mineral solution only or in an inert medium such as perlite, rock-wool, cocopeat.
  • As farming is done in a controlled environment, all year-round cultivation is possible.

How Does Hydroponic Produce Compare To Organic And Conventional Produce?

Hydroponic produce is:

  • More hygienic
  • Free from soil-borne diseases or contaminants
  • High nutritional value
  • In organic we feed the soil, in hydroponics we feed the plant.

What is Non-GMO?

GMO (Genetically modified organisms) can be defined as organisms (plants, animals, etc) in which the genetic material (DNA) has been altered in a way that does not occur naturally.

At Nature’s Miracle, we only use Non-GMO seeds.

What makes Super Sweet Snack Peppers Sweet?

Super Sweet Snack Peppers contain higher levels of fruit sugar and lesser levels of capsaicin (hotness alkaloid) which makes it super sweet. The Snack peppers go through the full process of ripening allowing the natural sugars to fully develop inside the fruit to give their unique sweet flavour.

Why Hydroponics?

  • 90% less water used for irrigation (re-circulation of water)
  • No pollution (soil, water, air)
  • Integrated Pest Management
  • Consistent results (crop/fruit size, shape, taste, colour)
  • Sustainable use of resources (rainwater harvesting, water re-circulation, minimal human resource, renewable substrates)
  • Automated climate control (Sensors for pH levels, temperature, humidity, CO2 levels, wind speed and direction, rainfall, radiation)
