What is Hydroponics?

Hydroponics is one of the most advanced ways of farming today. In simple terms, it issoilless farming, where plants are grown in a controlled, environment friendly and sustainable eco system. The word “Hydroponics" is an amalgamation of two Greek words: "hydro", meaning water and "ponic" meaning labour or literally, water working.

It is a method of growing plants in a nutrient-rich water. Plants are grown with their roots in a highly absorbent inert medium such as perlite, rockwool and cocopeat.This inert media mimics soil allowing roots to anchor and is customized for different crop requirements. It pro-actively rules out any soil or groundwater borne contamination or pests and weeds. This in turn reduces requirement for pesticides or other interventions. Simultaneously, the ‘customized’ medium that is hosting the roots is conducive for providing adequate and high-quality nutrition for the plants.

Here, the highly qualified growers are sufficiently equipped withacademic prowess and deep-running experience with the respective crops to set the protocols for various growing manoeuvres and prompt (at times very subtle) responses which enhance and maintain the quality of produce. In rare instances this even averts unforeseen disasters through timely and apt interventions and directions. The resources are marshalled very judiciously as our philosophy focuses on feeding the plants rather than feeding soil.

The Nature’s Miracle Way

Ideal Climate and nutrition
Our fully automated greenhouse is equipped with the latest and most advanced technology to control and optimize temperature, humidity, optimal nutrition and frequency for irrigation and many more parameters to create an ideal growing micro-environment for the respective crops. Each crop gets the ideal climate conditions and nutrition suited for the healthiest crop. We follow the latest internationally integrated pest control protocol in our enclosed greenhouse to ensure the outcome in terms of quality produce.

Zero wastage
Only 100% reverse-osmosis water is used in our hydroponic system. We follow a zero wastage policy and reuse water after UV disinfection. In this way, our hydroponic farm only uses 10% of the water compared to a normal farm. Furthermore, since it is a closed system, nutrients do not leach away – therefore making it possible to use a fraction (~25%) of the fertiliser used by a regular farm. Moreover, no fertilizer finds it way to the soil presenting any risk of soil or groundwater contamination even in the long run.

Efficient logistics
By responsibly planning the produce and with the help of an efficient post-harvest best practices, reducing transport emissions by way of more efficient logistics and providing people with fresher goods makes us proud.
